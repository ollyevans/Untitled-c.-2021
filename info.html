When you look at something small like a flower, and properly look at it, for a moment it is your world and you become completely immersed. I aim to photograph flowers in a way to let you experience them as I do.

The ideas this project went through range from ideas such as the lifespan of a single flower, a flower deconstructed, hue shifted flowers, and varying arrangements. Different backdrops were considered and lighting arrangements but were disregarded as they drew away from the original experience.

These photos were taken from a close distance, under natural light, with both manual and automatic focus. The photos have a wide aperture for a directed focus, a medium shutter to capture intricate details and ISO of 400 for rich blacks.

The photos were shot and cropped to a square-format, intending to lead the viewer's eyes in a circle with a focus on central composition.
